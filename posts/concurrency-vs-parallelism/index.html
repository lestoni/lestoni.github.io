Concurrency vs Parallelism

Nov 22, 2016 14:10 · 96 words · 1 minute read Concurrency Distributed Systems

Difference between concurrency and parallelism does not resonate with most people, including me, for a while now. Until i started learning Go and everything became clear. Particularly with this one concise explaination that has stuck with me:

Concurrency is a way to structure a program by breaking it into pieces that can be executed independently. Parallelism is about running those pieces simultaneously.
